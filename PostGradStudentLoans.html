Paying Back Your Student Loans Post-Graduation

*all information from studentaid.gov

Commonly Asked Questions

When do I need to pay back my student loans?

You should begin paying your loans when you graduate, drop below half-time enrollment or leave school. Most of time, there is a six month grace period after one of these events in which you are required to start repaying your loan. PLUS loans do not have this grace period.

What payment options do I have?

Your loan provider will provide you with a payment plan.

Repayment Plan Eligible Loans Monthly Payment
Standard Repayment Plan

- Direct Subsidized and Unsubsidized Loans

- Subsidized and Unsubsidized Federal Stafford Loans

- all PLUS loans

- all Consolidation Loans (Direct or FFEL)

Payments are a fixed amount that ensures your loans are paid off within 10 years

Graduated Repayment Plan

- Direct Subsidized and Unsubsidized Loans

- Subsidized and Unsubsidized Federal Stafford Loans

- all PLUS loans

- all Consolidation Loans (Direct or FFEL)

Payments are lower at first and then increase, usually every two years, and are for an amount that will ensure your loans are paid off within 10 years

Extended Repayment Plan

- Direct Subsidized and Unsubsidized Loans

- Subsidized and Unsubsidized Federal Stafford Loans

- all PLUS loans

- all Consolidation Loans (Direct or FFEL)

Payments may be fixed or graduated, and will ensure that your loans are paid off within 25 years.

If none of these options work for you, go to studentaid.gov studentaid.gov/manage for more options or talk to your loan provider.

How do I set up a payment plan?

Your loan provider will contact you and set you up under the Standard Repayment Plan unless you choose a different plan.

If you schedule an automatic monthly electronic debit of your loan payment from your checking or savings account, you receive a 0.25% interest rate deduction on Direct Loans. You can make payments by mail too.

Having trouble paying back your loans?

Contact your provider immediately. You may be able to change your repayment plan to one that lowers your monthly payment and, in some cases, may be based on your income.
