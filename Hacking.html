Here is a quick overview on what is financial aid

Financial aid is a way to help prospective college students to pay for their secondary education.

Aid can come in four forms, such as:

  • scholarships
  • grants
  • loans
  • work study

Aid does not require payback, unless it is a loan. Otherwise, it is extra pocket money or money to help pay fundings

The sources of the aid are the federal government, New York State, colleges offering aid, and organizations/associations and other groups looking to help college students finacially.

Learn more on how financial aid is awarded! The way it is determined is by looking at the cost of attendance, how much attending the college is, then subtracting the amount of expected family contribution, how much your family can pay.
